Projects

PhoPhyClear(PI):
Coupling photocatalysis and membrane technologies for the removal of microplastics from drinking water in remote areas: Combining microfiltration and advance oxidation processes, we will develop a proof-of-concept process that can be applied in remote areas and remove most types of microplastics efficiently, independently of the size and shape. (Funding body: GCRF). Treatment of microplastics in wastewater(Co-I):
The project aims to develop a high surface area oxidation or reforming catalyst for the treatment of dry sludge; to examine the effect of metal, N and P containing compounds on the catalytic treatment; and to assess the ability to separate the catalyst from the treated sludge and examine its recyclability. (Founding body: UK Catalysis Hub). Collaborations: Prof Chris Hardacre (PI) (University of Manchester, UK), Prof Davide Mattia (University of Bath, UK).
